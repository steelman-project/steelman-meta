## What was built

The first politically-matched evaluation dataset for the Tier 1 detectors: 42 examples, 21 per direction, mirror-paired by category. All six Tier 1 categories represented at 3 mirror-paired positive examples per category plus 3 mirror-paired negative examples (policy-position-with-rationale posts that should not fire any pattern). Examples were LLM-synthesized (Claude Opus 4.7) in the voices of generic center-left and center-right American political speakers (senator, advocate, official) and labeled by a single maintainer.

## What evaluation was done

One full pass on a fresh cache (280 cache misses, 11 incidental hits from prior `analyze` calls). 291 model calls total. Wall time ~14 minutes. The generated report's `Verdict: FAIL` reflects one symmetric-application threshold breach (`self_contradiction`); other findings of detector-quality concern are documented below as "secondary findings" rather than threshold breaches.

## Threshold-breach investigation

- **Category:** `self_contradiction`
- **Direction with higher detection rate:** right (2/6 fires vs 0/6 left)
- **Hypothesized cause:** detector prompt bias toward universal-claim-vs-counterexample contradictions, blind to principle-vs-action contradictions. The right-direction positives are structurally vivid universal claims that the speaker immediately contradicts ("every regulation kills jobs / this one creates jobs"). The left-direction positives are subtler stated-principle / proposed-action mismatches ("no tax under $400K / payroll surcharge on every worker"). The detector caught both right examples that hit the universal-vs-counterexample shape and missed all four (across both directions) that don't.
- **Evidence for hypothesis:** Spot-check — right TPs are `ex-r-self-contradiction-2` ("every regulation kills jobs" / "this zoning rule creates thousands") and `ex-r-self-contradiction-3` ("government should never pick winners and losers" / "guarantee tax credits only for industries we prefer"). Both feature explicit universal quantifiers ("every", "never") followed by a self-counterexample. The four false negatives are all principle/action mismatches with no universal quantifier in the contradicted clause.
- **Remediation plan:** Tune the `self_contradiction` detector prompt to recognize principle-vs-action mismatches in addition to universal-vs-counterexample shapes. Owner: Aaron Caswell. Target re-evaluation date: as part of Phase 6 detector-tuning iteration (no fixed date — dependent on Phase 5b approval).
- **Tracking issue:** none yet — captured here as the remediation source of truth.

**Caveat on the breach.** With N=6 per direction and a single labeler, the 33% asymmetry could be partially attributable to labeling judgment ("I called these contradictions but the engine sees them as scope ambiguity") rather than detector bias. Real-world data with two-labeler review (see Known limitations) is required to attribute the breach with confidence to the detector.

## Secondary findings (not threshold breaches but engine-quality issues)

These do not block Phase 5b approval but are tracked as Phase 6 detector-tuning work:

1. **`claim_burst` fires 0 times across 8 positive examples (0% recall).** Posts contain 5 stacked claims; detector should fire. The detector's symmetric-application metric is vacuously balanced (0/0). Likely cause: detector prompt requires more structural markers than just claim-count, or the detector confidence is being suppressed below an internal floor despite the `confidence_threshold` shipping at 0.0. Engine bug, not a labeling artifact.

2. **`minors` refusal class fires on general child-care policy speech.** Two examples (`ex-l-no-support-3` "Child care subsidies pay for themselves..."; `ex-l-negative-2` "I support the child care pilot...") were refused as minor-related content. The refusal class is designed to filter out posts identifying individual minors, not policy discussion mentioning "child care." Refusal-layer false-positive bug; over-broad pattern matching needs narrowing.

3. **`no_support_provided` spurious firings on posts labeled for other categories** (e.g. `ex-r-self-contradiction-2` fires both `no_support_provided` and `self_contradiction`). These are technically correct calls — the posts ARE unsupported — but show up as detector-rate noise because the dataset only labels the "primary" pattern per post. This is the label-count asymmetry flagged in the dataset's `inclusion_criteria` showing up exactly as predicted.

## What is ready for sibling repos

This evaluation confirms the following capabilities are usable by sibling repos:

- End-to-end pipeline (ingest → extract → detect → graph → assemble) runs on real LLM calls without infrastructure errors.
- Provenance is attached to every output (rubric version, model identity, input hash, cache hits, supporting spans, rubric clause IDs).
- Cache layer reuses extract + detect calls on cache hits.
- Cost telemetry produces per-stage USD figures correctly.
- `explicit_refusal`, `yes_no_non_answer`, and `green_flags` detectors are production-quality on this dataset (≥83% accuracy, 100% precision on the first two; 83% precision on green_flags).

NOT confirmed by this evaluation and explicitly deferred:

- `self_contradiction` detector quality (threshold breach + 33% recall).
- `no_support_provided` detector quality (50% recall).
- `claim_burst` detector functionality (0% recall — broken).
- Refusal layer specificity (`minors` over-fires).
- Engine behavior on natural, non-LLM-generated discourse.

## Known limitations

- **Single-labeler.** Labels were authored solo, violating the `evaluation/README.md` "Adding examples" requirement for two-maintainer review from different political starting points. A two-labeler matched dataset is tracked as the next validation cycle's primary deliverable.
- **LLM-synthesized examples.** The same model family (Claude) that generated the example text performs detection. Symmetric-application results on this dataset show the engine treats its own writing style symmetrically; they do NOT generalize to natural discourse.
- **Small N.** 6 examples per direction per category is too few to draw confident detector-quality conclusions. The threshold-breach hypothesis above is provisional pending a larger real-world dataset.
- **Label-count asymmetry.** Two left-side `no_support_provided` examples (`ex-l-no-support-1`, `ex-l-no-support-3`) are dual-labeled with `claim_burst`. The mirrored right-side `no_support_provided` examples make fewer claims and are single-labeled. Documented in the dataset's `inclusion_criteria` field; surfaces as the higher `claim_burst` positive count (8 left labels vs 6 right labels) in the report.
